Best AR Glasses Right Now (Quick Catalog)

This page is just a simple, opinionated catalog for a few AR glasses that currently stand out for media consumption and “virtual monitor” use, rather than full-blown mixed reality.

RayNeo Air 3S

Lightweight Good for movies

RayNeo Air 3S are focused on being light and comfortable while still giving you a bright virtual screen. They are a solid option if your priority is watching content on the go without a lot of extra setup.

Highlights

  • Lightweight design, easier to wear for long sessions.
  • Good brightness for indoor and dim outdoor use.
  • Comfortable for travel, flights, and bed watching.

Use Case

Good choice if you mainly want a “portable cinema” style experience and don’t need a lot of advanced features or heavy tinkering.

XREAL One

Popular Good software ecosystem

XREAL One is part of the XREAL family that’s become very popular with people who want a large virtual screen for laptops, handheld consoles, and phones.

Highlights

  • Strong ecosystem with XREAL’s software and accessories.
  • Good option for using as an external monitor replacement.
  • Works well with many devices via USB-C / adapters.

Use Case

Choose XREAL One if you want something that “just works” with a lot of setups and you care about software support and updates.

XREAL One Pro

Premium Better image / features

XREAL One Pro is the higher-end variant aimed at people who want better image quality and extra features compared to the base One.

Highlights

  • Improved visuals compared to the non-Pro model.
  • Better fit if you’re picky about clarity and detail.
  • Still part of the same XREAL ecosystem and apps.

Use Case

If you’re already leaning towards XREAL but want the “better” version and you’re fine paying more, the Pro is usually the one to look at first.
